Gilgamesh

Gilgamesh was a major hero in ancient Mesopotamian mythology and the protagonist of the Epic of Gilgamesh. He was a historical king of the Sumerian city-state of Uruk, who was posthumously deified

Enkidu

Enkidu was wartime comrade and friend of Gilgamesh, king of Uruk. He is the oldest literary representation of the wild man, a recurrent motif in artistic representations in Mesopotamia and in Ancient Near East literature

Utnapishtim

Utnapishtim is a character in ancient Mesopotamian mythology. He is tasked by the god Enki (Ea) to create a giant ship to be called Preserver of Life in preparation of a giant flood that would wipe out all life.

Shamhat

She is a sacred prostitute who plays a significant role in bringing the wild man Enkidu into contact with civilization.

Humbaba

Humbaba or "the Terrible", was a monstrous giant of immemorial age raised by Utu, the Sun. Humbaba was the guardian of the Cedar Forest, where the gods lived, by the will of the god Enlil, who "assigned Humbaba as a terror to human beings". Gilgamesh and Enkidu defeated this great enemy

Ishtar

Ishtar wanted Gilgamesh to be her husband, but he refused. Feeling humiliated, she sent the Bull of Haeven to punish Gilgamesh. Gilgamesh and Enkidu killed the Bull of Haeven, causing the gods to decide to condemn Enkidu to death.
